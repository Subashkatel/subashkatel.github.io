I am a final-year Computer Science student at UCSD. Currently, I am applying to graduate school to explore my passion for compilers, machine learning, and heterogeneous systems.

My research interests focus on leveraging compilers and machine learning techniques to optimize heterogeneous systems for high-performance computing environments. I have had the chance contribute to developing compiler tools and techniques that bridge the gap between high-level machine learning models and hardware-specific optimizations.

In addition to my work in computer science, I have had chance to contribute in high-energy physics, applying self-supervised learning methods to analyze particle jet data. My physics research, under the mentorship of Prof. Javier Duarte, led to the development of the Jet-based Joint Embedding Predictive Architecture (J-JEPA), a framework for learning augmentation-independent representations of jets.
